Malay Heritage

Samporna Spa

The traditional Malay healing art of Urut

Samporna draws from the Malay tradition of Urut Melayu — a system of deep therapeutic massage that focuses on energy pathways known as urat and stimulates circulation through rhythmic pressure. Our Samporna therapists are trained in the oral tradition, passing down techniques through direct mentorship.

The treatment uses warmed coconut oil infused with halia (ginger), kunyit (turmeric), and serai (lemongrass) — all sourced from Kedah and Kelantan. The session closes with a traditional jampi-style hot compress.

Ancient Chinese

Qin Spa

Meridian therapy rooted in Tui Na practice

The Qin tradition is built on the principles of Traditional Chinese Medicine — specifically Tui Na, a form of therapeutic bodywork that works through the body's meridian channels to restore the flow of qi. Each session begins with a brief assessment of constitution before the therapist selects the appropriate sequence of strokes and pressure points.

The Qin experience does not use oil — instead, practitioners work through a light draping cloth, allowing precision of pressure without the warmth of botanicals. It is most recommended for those managing chronic muscle tension, fatigue, or poor circulation.

Before Your Visit

How to Prepare for Your Treatment

Arrive 15 minutes early

Allow time to settle in the lounge, change, and have a brief consultation with your therapist before the session begins.

Avoid heavy meals 2 hours prior

A light meal is fine; a full stomach is uncomfortable during deep tissue work. Hydrate well before and after your session.

Communicate your needs

Let your therapist know of any injuries, sensitivities, or areas requiring particular attention. Pressure and technique can be adjusted throughout.

Reschedule if unwell

Massage therapy is contraindicated during fever, acute infection, or inflamed skin conditions. Call us to reschedule — we will accommodate you.
