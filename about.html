Deluxcious · Our Story

A Mansion Restored

From a forgotten colonial estate to one of George Town's most celebrated heritage destinations — the story of Deluxcious spans more than 140 years.

The Building

Built 1880. Restored 2014–2019.

The building at 15-17A Jalan Sultan Ahmad Shah was originally constructed in the late 1880s as a private residence for a prominent Straits Chinese merchant family. Its Straits Eclectic architecture — a fusion of European neoclassical design with Chinese decorative motifs — was characteristic of the wealthy merchant class of late-19th century Penang.

The property changed hands several times through the 20th century, serving variously as a boarding house, private offices, and most recently, as a partially tenanted residential building. By the early 2000s, much of the structure had fallen into disrepair — though the essential bones of the building remained intact.

In 2014, the property was acquired with the sole intention of a faithful restoration. Penang Heritage Trust consultants were engaged alongside master craftsmen from within the George Town conservation community. Every original element — the hand-laid Peranakan mosaic floors, the carved timber balustrades, the cast-iron ventilation grilles — was assessed, repaired where possible, and replaced only where structural integrity required it.

Original Construction

Built for a Straits Chinese merchant family. Straits Eclectic architecture, hand-laid mosaic floors, carved timber throughout.

1940
Wartime & Post-War Transitions

The building served as temporary wartime accommodation during the Japanese occupation, then as a boarding house in the years that followed.

2008
UNESCO Heritage Designation

George Town inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. The building falls within the heritage core zone, increasing both its protection and significance.

2014
Restoration Begins

Five-year restoration project commissioned. Penang Heritage Trust consultants and master craftsmen engaged. Original structural survey identifies 94% of original fabric salvageable.

2019
Deluxcious Opens

18 heritage suites, three spa sanctuaries, and the restaurant open their doors. The building has its new purpose — one it was perhaps always suited for.

The Chef

Chef Keith Khoo

Executive Chef & Culinary Director

Born and raised in Penang, Keith Khoo spent his formative years cooking alongside his grandmother — a Nyonya cook whose recipes drew from three generations of Peranakan tradition. That foundation never left him.

After formal training at Le Cordon Bleu in Sydney, Keith spent a decade in Michelin-starred kitchens across London and Singapore — most notably as sous chef at the Ritz-Carlton Singapore's fine dining programme. He returned to Penang in 2016 to begin work on Deluxcious, spending three years developing a menu that could honour the building's heritage while standing firmly in its own right.

His cooking resists simple categorisation. European precision, Southeast Asian instinct, and a deep sensitivity to local produce — each dish is a considered conversation between tradition and craft.

What We Believe

Our Philosophy

Four principles guide everything we do — from how we restore a room to how we plate a dish.

Heritage First

Every decision begins with the building. We do not renovate — we restore. The past is the foundation, not the obstacle.

Craft Over Speed

Five years to restore. Hours to prepare a bisque. No shortcuts in the kitchen, and none in the building work either.

Local Roots

We source from Penang market suppliers, Camerons Highland farms, and local producers wherever possible. The region feeds us.

Guest Before Guest

We consider the experience before the service interaction. What does a guest feel when they walk through the door? That question shapes every detail.
